1913
Henry Ford introduces flow production at his Highland Park plant in Michigan. By arranging the work steps in a logical sequence, he makes it possible to build cars much faster than before.
1918 to 1935
Sakichi Toyoda begins with an automatic loom invention. Over the years, the company shifted toward making cars, eventually becoming Toyota.
1950
When Eiji Toyoda and Taiichi Ohno visited American factories, they noticed gaps in efficiency and came back to Japan with ideas for upgrading Toyota’s workflow.
1965
Toyota rolls out its Production System to suppliers and starts documenting how it works.
1973
The 1970s oil crisis revealed Toyota's efficiency compared to traditional American automakers.
1990
The book "The Machine That Changed the World" comes out and popularizes the word “Lean.”
2000s to Now
Lean ideas have spread into many different areas, including software, healthcare, retail, and a variety of other industries, reaching far beyond car manufacturing.
